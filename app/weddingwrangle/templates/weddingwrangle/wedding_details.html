Directions

Childerley is a fifteen minute drive from the centre of Cambridge. Panther Taxis and Uber both serve the area. The wedding weekend coincides with the University of Cambridge's graduation ceremonies, so we recommend booking a taxi for your return journey in advance.

There is plenty of parking available on-site. The site can be accessed from the A428 / St Neots Road between Caldecote and Bourn.

About Childerley

Childerley Hall is a Grade II listed country house built in the late 16th century for the fourth Sir John Cutts. The surviving wing contains the King Charles Chamber, named after Charles I who stayed there after his arrest by Parliament forces at the apotheosis of the First English Civil War. For further reading on the period, Beccy’s undergraduate dissertation (2012) can be provided on request.
